The idea

Do not gut the church. Give it a credible working life.

Pigtown Sanctuary is the working name for a preservation/adaptive-use concept centered on the historic church at 527 Scott Street. The core idea is simple: preserve the sanctuary as the main space, keep the church identity legible, and use family stewardship, arts, music, and carefully vetted community activity to support the building over time.

This is not presented as a completed project, approved use, purchased property, or public venue. It is a serious dream-stage feasibility effort that needs the right Baltimore professionals, written city answers, and a practical plan before anything is represented as final.

What the building could support

Church and sanctuary use

Continued religious, reflective, musical, and gathering use, with the sanctuary preserved as the building’s defining space.

Family residence and caretaking

Possible parsonage, caretaker, or family living space if Baltimore confirms the use in writing and the code path is workable.

Music, arts, and making

Workshops for stained glass, lamp making, welding, theater build, lighting, sound, and small-scale creative work tied to the building’s preservation.

Guiding principles

Keep the sanctuary as the heart of the property.
Preserve the front, the crest, the pediment, and the visible historic character.
Use new functions only where they support the building’s survival.
Be transparent about zoning, residential use, fire/code, structural, parking, events, and occupancy questions.
Build support slowly, honestly, and with people who can actually say yes or no.
Current status: exploratory. No final purchase, use, zoning approval, occupancy approval, residential arrangement, public opening, nonprofit/church structure, or event operation is represented by this site.
