A Baltimore sanctuary idea

A church kept alive, not erased.

Pigtown Sanctuary is a first sketch of a family dream: preserve a historic church, keep the sanctuary as the soul of the building, and imagine a long-term home for music, worship, making, teaching, repair, family, and neighborhood connection.

The dream starts with the existing building: brick, sanctuary, memory, music, and the feeling that this place should not be lost.
The basic idea

Sanctuary first. Family stewardship around it.

A place that still feels like a church, with life and care around the edges.

This is not a finished plan. It is a way to hold the idea in one place while we think, talk, look, listen, and decide whether the dream deserves the next step.

The center of the idea is simple: do not gut the sanctuary, do not flatten the history, and do not turn the building into something generic. Preserve the main space and let everything else serve that.

Vision board

Sanctuary

The room stays sacred

Music, reflection, worship, readings, quiet, and gatherings in the space that gives the building its meaning.

Family

Long-term care

A family-led stewardship project, not a quick flip. A place watched, repaired, loved, and slowly brought back.

Making

Workshops and craft

Stained glass, lighting, welding, design, theater work, repair, and the kind of making that belongs in an old building.

Community

Open when it makes sense

Small moments of neighborhood life: music, teaching, listening, youth arts, open studio days, and careful hospitality.

A note of caution

Still just an idea.

This is only a public-facing vision board for a preservation dream. It is not an offer, opening announcement, approval, or final plan.
