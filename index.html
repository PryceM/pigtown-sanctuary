The mission

Do not turn the building into something generic.

The building should stay recognizably alive as a church, with the sanctuary preserved as the main space.

This project is being approached as a retirement-scale family stewardship effort, not a flip and not a generic event-hall concept. The strongest version keeps the church identity honest: worship, gathering, music, teaching, repair, family caretaking, and community connection all have to support the survival of the sanctuary.

The public question is not “How do we get around zoning?” It is: Can this historic church continue as a bona fide religious/community property, and can any limited residential, parsonage, caretaker, or family-living component be confirmed in writing?

What the site is meant to do

The verification path

The next answer has to come from the city in writing.

Zoning use. Confirm whether 527 Scott Street can remain a church/religious-use property and what use category applies.
Residential/parsonage/caretaker use. Confirm whether any family living, parsonage, caretaker, or accessory residential component is allowed and under what conditions.
Use and occupancy. Confirm what certificate, change-of-use review, or occupancy classification would be required before any part of the building is occupied or opened.
Code, fire, and accessibility. Identify the practical triggers: egress, sprinklers, alarms, assembly limits, bathrooms, accessibility, MEP systems, and life-safety upgrades.
Preservation and exterior work. Verify whether historic review applies to exterior changes and what can be repaired, restored, or added without damaging the building’s character.

What it could become, if verified

Sanctuary-centered church use

Worship, reflection, music, readings, meetings, and small community gatherings inside a preserved sanctuary.

Family stewardship and caretaking

Active on-site care, repair, security, and presence, with any parsonage or family-living concept treated as a legal question to be answered before design.

Mission-related arts and teaching

Music, stained glass, lighting, fabrication, teaching, youth programming, and creative work only where they support the building’s church/community identity.

What this is not

No fake certainty. No fake church. No public promises.

This site does not represent that a purchase has been completed, that zoning has been approved, that occupancy has been granted, that residential use is allowed, or that the building is open to the public. It is a serious preservation inquiry and a way to gather the right guidance before money, design, or family expectations get ahead of the facts.
