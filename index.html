A historic sanctuary for a living mission

Preserving the sanctuary. Keeping the mission alive.

Pigtown Sanctuary is a family-led church-preservation and adaptive-use vision for a historic Baltimore sanctuary. The goal is to keep the building recognizably alive as a church, with the sanctuary intact as the heart of the property and future use grounded in worship, music, family stewardship, arts, gathering, and neighborhood connection.

Contact Pigtown SanctuaryRead the vision

The idea

Do not erase the church. Preserve the sanctuary and give the building a credible working life.

The strongest adaptive-use projects keep the defining space intact and let new functions support the building rather than replace its identity. For Pigtown Sanctuary, that means the sanctuary remains the main space, and any future family, caretaker, studio, workshop, arts, or gathering use must support the building’s religious and community character.

What it could become

Continued church and gathering use

A preserved sanctuary for worship, reflection, music, readings, meetings, and community connection.

Family stewardship and caretaking

A practical plan for keeping the building active, watched, repaired, and loved, with possible parsonage, caretaker, or family living space if confirmed in writing by the city.

Arts, music, and making

A place where performance, stained glass, lighting, design, small-scale fabrication, and creative work fit naturally inside a preserved historic setting.

Guiding principles

Keep the sanctuary as the heart of the project.
Preserve the church identity instead of converting the building into something generic.
Use new functions to support the building’s survival.
Confirm zoning, residential/parsonage/caretaker use, code, fire, and occupancy issues in writing before making promises.
Build support carefully, honestly, and without pretending the project is further along than it is.
